About

The Miyawaki Method is a revolutionary afforestation technique developed by Japanese botanist Dr. Akira Miyawaki, aimed at restoring natural ecosystems by planting dense, native forests. At IIT Palakkad, this method is adopted to create a self-sustaining green space that contributes to biodiversity, improves air quality, and fosters environmental awareness. Unlike traditional plantations, Miyawaki forests grow 10 times faster, are 30 times denser, and require minimal maintenance after the initial 2–3 years. The forest at our campus exemplifies the institute's commitment to eco-conscious living and sustainability.

🌱 Our Vision

To redefine campus sustainability by cultivating a thriving, native forest that serves as a living laboratory and a symbol of ecological responsibility.

🎯 Our Mission

At IIT Palakkad, we aim to nurture biodiversity and build a resilient ecosystem through Miyawaki plantations. Our on-campus forest houses around 1200 trees of various native species, whose growth is continuously tracked and studied. Every year, first-year B.Tech students visit the site as part of their Ecology and Environment course to measure tree growth, helping calculate biomass and understand forest dynamics.

✨ Key Features

  • ✅ Native species preservation
  • ⚡ Rapid forest development
  • 🌿 Biodiversity enhancement
  • 🌍 Carbon sequestration
  • 📚 Hands-on learning for students

🧑‍🔬 Student Involvement

The Miyawaki forest is also an interactive learning space. Students participate in the following activities:

  • 🌱 Tree height & girth measurements
  • 🌳 Species monitoring
  • 📊 Data collection for biomass estimation
  • 🌿 Research on native flora and climate adaptation
